Research activities focus on antimicrobial pharmacology, infectious disease epidemiology, and clinical pharmacokinetics. Current work emphasizes optimization of antimicrobial therapy, characterization of PK/PD variability, and identification of clinical and environmental determinants of antimicrobial resistance. Quantitative methodologies—including meta-analysis, population pharmacokinetic (PopPK) modeling, real-world evidence analysis, and causal inference—are applied to support individualized dosing strategies and evidence-based antimicrobial stewardship.

Published research includes multiple first-author articles in internationally peer-reviewed journals spanning pharmacology, infectious diseases, and environmental health. Long-term objectives involve integrating translational pharmacology with data-driven infectious disease research to improve therapeutic decision-making for severe and drug-resistant infections.
